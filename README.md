# Project Layman :)

> _**layman**_ _(noun)_  
> _someone who is not trained in or does not have a detailed knowledge of a particular subject_  
> _synonyms: noob, normie_  

In "the age of AI", it is so easy for FOMO to settle in when you see people around you just constantly "building things over the weekend", or even "over the afternoon".
You also hear people claim having "swarms of AI building in their sleep". 
You don't often know what exactly they are building, but you just know that they are building, and you are feeling FOMO anyways.
So you set out to try out these "efficiency gains". But very soon you realize that there is just so much "noise" all around you.

How do I even start?

Glad you asked. Welcome to Project Layman. Yet another "playbook" to add upon the noise. But here are my two cents on the topic anyways.
As the name suggests, this project is designed as a guide for people who have no programming experience whatsoever.
While there are people obsessed with AI, and also people who absolutely hate AI, you can't deny that AI can really get certain things done really quickly.
Whether those things are done well, or whether those things should be done in the first place, those are certainly up for discussion.
But one thing I do see is that AI could act as an enabler. It could democratizes certain skills, that are often gatekeeped by people who define their whole identity over said skills.
Personally, I am all about helping people succeed. I love seeing people build cool shit. Its awesome to see people make things that they used to only dream about.
And I want to be a part of that. Hence this project.
